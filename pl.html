Europejski Zielony Ład


Europejski Zielony Ład


In December 2019, the Green Deal for Europe strategy was developed for the European Union's climate policy.

Do najważniejszych celów należą:

  • adopting a binding climate neutrality target by 2050,
  • revision of the short-term target - the 2030 emissions reduction target is currently 40% compared to 1990 levels, but is planned to be increased to 50 or 55%.

The European Green Deal provides an action plan to:

  • boost the efficient use of resources by moving to a clean, circular economy,
  • restore biodiversity and cut pollution.
The theme of urban green management is inextricably linked to European strategy documents setting climate targets – achieving climate neutrality by 2050 and reducing emissions by 2030.  The European Union's Green Deal for Europe climat policy envisages achieving these objectives through efficient use of resources by moving to a clean, circular economy; cut pollution; but also through biodiversity restore. 

EU Biodiversity Strategy for 2030


The Green Deal assumes the synergy of actions to combat the climate crisis, among others through actions to protect biodiversity, presented in the EU Biodiversity strategy until 2030.

EU Nature Restoration Plan (key commitments by 2030) includes Urban Greening Plan for cities with at least 20,000 inhabitants:

These should include measures to create biodiverse and accessible urban forests, parks and gardens; urban farms; green roofs and walls; treelined streets; urban meadows; and urban hedges.

They should also help improve connections between green spaces, eliminate the use of pesticides, limit excessive mowing of urban green spaces and other biodiversity harmful practices.

It thus refers to actions to protect biodiversity, presented in the EU Biodiversity strategy until 2030.   What does it look like in practice?  EU Nature Restoration Plan assumes that Cities with at least 20,000 inhabitants will develop Urban Greening Plans containing measures to create biodiverse and accessible urban forests, parks and gardens and other forms of urban greenery, as well as improve connections between green spaces, and eliminate biodiversity harmful practice like a f.e. use of pesticides or too frequent mowing of urban green spaces. 

Bioróżnorodność i zmiany klimatu


The strategy aims to reverse these trends and stop the loss of green urban ecosystems as well as adaptation and mitigation to climate change by:

  • promoting healthy ecosystems,
  • green infrastructure,
  • nature-based solutions.

In order to stop the loss of green urban ecosystems while using the properties of urban greenery for adaptation and mitigation to climate change, measures are proposed to promote healthy ecosystems, i.e. the protection of natural natural systems located in the city tissue and wide range of nature-based solutions, including green infrastructure. 

Ecosystem services

Ecosystem services provided by urban vegetation


Microclimate control


Trees reduce the scope of heat island in the summer period as the water evaporation from leaves is cooling the air.

LA deflect sun radiation (high albedo), which otherwise could be absorbed by impervious surfaces (low albedo).

Temperature reduction:

  • Cooling (Kwh)
  • Pollution Avoided

UV radiation protection


Trees offer protection from harmful UV.

Tree leaves absorb around 95% of ultraviolet radiation.

Stormwater control


Trees are increasingly recognized for their importance in managing runoff:

  • Reducing erosion caused by falling rain
  • Providing surface area where rain water lands and evaporates
  • Creating conditions in the soil that promote infiltration

Air purification


Trees breath in harmful for humans gasses like NO2 , SO2 , O3 or CO and capture PM.

Large trees absorb many times more pollution that small trees.

Tunnelling effect.

Energy conservation


Trees provide shade

Trees block wind

Energy conservation:

  • Heating (MBTU and Kwh)
  • Cooling (Kwh)
  • Pollution Avoided

Sources:

  • Satellites, planes and drones

  • An image of the Earth's surface captured by remote sensors

Benefits:

  • Cheap
  • Quick
  • Spatially continous
  • Objective
  • Current and collected regularly at short intervals

Various platforms and sensors used for remote sensing (Source: Research Gate)

Advantages of using remote sensing data


  1. Earth observation data - unique source of information. Earth observation can provide a unique and timely source of data that is commensurable across countries, regions and cities.
  2. It can be combined with administrative, social and economic data at multiple scales for in-depth policy analysis.
  3. It can help harmonise reporting on natural resources, ecosystems and environmental risks.
  4. Remote sensing allows to capture an image of a large area of the Earth in short intervals and relatively cheaply. The image is spatially continuous.
  5. Information obtained from Earth observation is objective and can be automatically classified.

Rozdzielczość przestrzenna


Rozdzielczość przestrzenna - the size of the smallest object (pixel) observed on the ground.

Object is detected if its equal or larger than the pixel size. The smaller objects are detectable as the average brightness of all objects within the pixel.

(Źródło: Radiant Earth Foundation)

Rozdzielczość spektralna


Rozdzielczość spektralna - refers to the spectral bandwidth and the number of bands.

The finer spectral resolution, the narrower the wavelength range for the particular band.

(Źródło: Radiant Earth Foundation)

Radiometric resolution


Radiometric resolution - the sensitivity to the magnitude of the electromagnetic energy. It describes the actual information content in an image, expressed in units of bits.

The finer the radiometric resolution, the more sensitive it is to detect small differences in reflected or emitted energy.

(Source: Research Gate)

Temporal resolution


Temporal resolution - frequency at which the same place is observed (revisit capability).

It is particularly important for change mapping.

(Source: Radiant Earth Foundation)

Sentinel-2


  • Wystrzelenie: 23 June 2015 (Sentinel-2A) and 7 March 2017 (Sentinel-2B)
  • Wysokość orbity: 799 km
  • Instrument: the Multi-Spectral Instrument (MSI)
  • Rozdzielczość przestrzenna: 10 m / 20 m / 60 m
  • Rozdzielczość spektralna: 13 spectral bands (VI/Red Edge/NIR/SWIR)
  • Rozdzielczość czasowa: 10 days (1 satellites) 5 days (1 satellite)
  • Wielkość sceny: 290 km
  • Główne zastosowania obejmują rolnictwo; monitorowanie ekosystemów lądowych; gospodarkę leśną; monitorowanie jakości wód śródlądowych i przybrzeżnych; mapowanie katastrof naturalnych i bezpieczeństwa cywilnego

Street Tree Layer


The Street Tree Layer (STL) is a separate layer from the Urban Atlas 2012 LU/LC layer produced within the level 1 urban mask for each FUA.

It includes contiguous rows or a patches of trees covering 500 m² or more and with a minimum width of 10 meter over "Artificial surfaces" (nomenclature class 1) inside FUA (i.e. rows of trees along the road network outside urban areas or forest adjacent to urban areas should not be included).

Application examples (4)

NDVI - Normalized Difference Vegetation Index

Effective for quantifying green vegetation. Positively correlated with vegetation greenness.

NDVI = (NIR – Red) / (NIR + R)
For Sentinel-2 the formula is:
(B8 - B4) / (B8 + B4)
where: B8 = 842 nm, B4 = 665 nm

NDVI range value is -1 to 1

NDVI value on 12.09.2018 (Source: Publication Warszawa z kosmosu)

Applications and limitations

Applications:

  • Identification and classification of different types of green areas
  • Estimation of green urban areas
  • Proximity of green urban areas
  • Change detection
  • Vegetation and water indices

Limitations:

  • Detailed attributes of the single tree (taxon, height, ...)
  • Trees health assessment (defoliation, discoloration)
